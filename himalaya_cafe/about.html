About The Himalaya


Reka Gawa, owner of Himalaya Cafe on South Clerk Street, moved from India at a young age. She'd always wanted to own a cafe and has spent her career working with restaurants, cafes and catering. In 2004 Reka met the Dali Lama in Scotland and they spoke briefly, an interaction that she recalls clearly. The Dali Lama encouraged her to start a center to remember her culture and share it with others. That was the birth of the Himalaya Cafe.

After much work the Himalaya Cafe has become a faithful representation of Rekas fondest Tibetan memories. Rich fragrances of incense and delicious cuisine fill the air. Plush pashminas and rugs adorn the walls and seating. Warm and welcoming smiles from Reka and her staff welcome patrons.

A small shop provides hand crafted goods from Tibet, India and Nepal. Wonderfully made woolens, incense sticks, Himalayan salt, carved items and more are sold here, helping support people in these regions.

Charity


At the Himalaya Cafe you have not only the chance to enjoy the delicious refreshments and homeley atmosphere, you can also help give a child a better future. Out the kindness of her heart, Reka supports schooling costs for many children in northern India.

Many of these smart little children would miss the opportunity to develop life skills for the modern era without access to schooling. Thanks to the kind-heartedness of people like Reka more of these young children can get schooling and have their needs taken care of.

You too can open up your generosity toward these children! Next time you're in The Himalaya, why not ask for details and see how you can help improve the prospects for a child.
